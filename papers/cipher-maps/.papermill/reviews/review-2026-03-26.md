## Summary

**Overall Assessment**: The paper presents a clean and well-motivated abstraction (cipher maps) that unifies hash-based approximate data structures, trapdoor functions, and homophonic substitution under a single four-property framework. The main results (composition theorem, space lower bound, space optimality of the Singular Hash Map) are correct under stated assumptions. The writing is clear, the notation is consistent, and the honest limitations sections are unusually candid. The paper is in good shape after the recent rebuild from the formalism document. Remaining issues are addressable without fundamental restructuring.

**Strengths**:
1. The four-property framework (totality, representation uniformity, correctness, composability) is a genuine conceptual contribution that provides a unified vocabulary for reasoning about hash-based cryptographic-adjacent data structures (novelty-assessor)
2. Three concrete instantiations (HashSet, entropy map, trapdoor Boolean algebra) from distinct domains demonstrate that the abstraction is not an empty generalization (novelty-assessor, methodology-auditor)
3. The composition theorem proof is correct and the connection to Bernoulli error accumulation is well-drawn (logic-checker)
4. The honest limitations sections -- marginal uniformity only, equality pattern leakage, what this framework is not -- set clear expectations and avoid overclaiming (prose-auditor)
5. The space lower bound proof cleanly decomposes into membership and value components with an entropy chain rule justification for additivity (logic-checker)
6. The bibliography is clean: all 16 entries are cited, no orphans, no undefined references (citation-verifier, format-validator)
7. The Related Work section effectively positions cipher maps against Bloom filters, perfect hashing, SSE, OPE, garbled circuits, FHE, and homophonic substitution (literature-context)

**Weaknesses**:
1. The composition theorem is presented as an equality in the theorem statement but a subsequent remark retracts it to an upper bound, creating confusion about which claim the paper actually makes (logic-checker)
2. The random oracle model is used throughout but never formally defined or cited (methodology-auditor, citation-verifier)
3. The trust model (Section 6) is formalized after the constructions (Section 5) reference it, creating a forward-reference problem (prose-auditor)
4. The Bayesian deniability proposition assumes symmetric error rates without stating the assumption (logic-checker)
5. The paper has zero figures despite 20 pages and existing TikZ figure files in the repository (prose-auditor, format-validator)
6. Shannon's source coding theorem and the random oracle model are referenced but not cited (citation-verifier)

## Major Issues

### M1. Composition theorem: equality vs. upper bound ambiguity (source: logic-checker)
- **Location**: Theorem 8.1 (lines 1059-1068), proof (lines 1070-1091), Remark 8.1 (lines 1093-1101)
- **Quoted text**: Theorem states "$\eta_{g \circ f} = 1 - (1-\eta_f)(1-\eta_g)$"; Remark says "The formula is therefore best understood as an upper bound under the weaker assumption that errors are positively correlated."
- **Problem**: The theorem presents the composition formula as an equality, but the proof requires a re-randomization assumption (condition (ii)) that does not hold in general for the hash-based constructions. The immediately following remark partially retracts the claim. A reader cannot tell whether the paper is claiming an equality or an inequality.
- **Suggestion**: Resolve the ambiguity by either: (a) stating the theorem as an inequality $\eta_{g \circ f} \leq \eta_f + \eta_g - \eta_f \eta_g$ under the weaker assumption, with a note that equality holds under re-randomization; or (b) keeping the equality but moving the re-randomization assumption into the theorem statement rather than burying it in the proof. Option (a) is cleaner.
- **Cross-verified**: Yes, by methodology-auditor. The re-randomization assumption is not satisfied by the SHM construction (which produces a specific encoding, not a uniformly random one). The remark correctly identifies this gap.

### M2. Random oracle model: used but not formally stated or cited (source: methodology-auditor, citation-verifier)
- **Location**: Definition 4.1 (line 293), Proposition 5.3 (line 665), Theorem 7.2 proof (line 963), and throughout
- **Quoted text**: "under the random oracle model" (appears 5 times without prior definition)
- **Problem**: The random oracle model is the foundation for most results in the paper (totality, false positive rates, construction time, space bounds). It is never formally defined and the standard reference (Bellare & Rogaway, 1993) is not cited. A reader must infer what "random oracle model" means from context.
- **Suggestion**: Add a notation/preliminaries paragraph to the end of Section 1 or the start of Section 3: "Throughout, $h : \{0,1\}^* \to \{0,1\}^n$ denotes a cryptographic hash function modeled as a random oracle [cite Bellare-Rogaway 1993]: a truly random function accessible to all parties via oracle queries. Results stated 'under the random oracle model' assume $h$ has this property."

### M3. Section ordering: trust model should precede constructions (source: prose-auditor)
- **Location**: Section 5 (Constructions) vs. Section 6 (Trust Model)
- **Problem**: The constructions in Section 5 repeatedly reference what the "untrusted machine" sees and what the "trusted machine" holds (e.g., lines 206-207: "The untrusted machine holds $\hat{f}$. The trusted machine holds enc, dec, and $s$."), but the trusted/untrusted machine model is not formalized until Section 6. This creates a forward-reference problem.
- **Suggestion**: Move Section 6 (Trust Model) to after Section 4 (Four Properties) and before Section 5 (Constructions). The natural flow is: abstraction -> properties -> trust model -> constructions -> singular hash map -> composition.
- **Cross-verified**: Yes, by novelty-assessor. The trust model is a framing contribution that helps the reader interpret the constructions; presenting it earlier strengthens the narrative.

## Minor Issues

### m1. Lower bound proof: informal $\varepsilon = n/|U|$ step (source: logic-checker)
- **Location**: Theorem 7.1 proof, line 883
- **Quoted text**: "Setting $\varepsilon = n/|U|$ (the fraction of the universe that decodes validly)"
- **Problem**: The identification of $\varepsilon$ with $n/|U|$ is informal. The paper defines $\varepsilon$ as the probability random bits form a valid codeword, which is $\sum_y |C_y| / 2^n$, not directly $n/|U|$. The step works but needs one sentence of justification.
- **Suggestion**: Add: "Any encoding where random $n$-bit strings decode to valid output with probability $\varepsilon$ implicitly defines an effective universe of size $1/\varepsilon$ per element, since at most a $\varepsilon$ fraction of inputs are valid."

### m2. Bayesian deniability assumes symmetric error (source: logic-checker)
- **Location**: Proposition 9.1, proof lines 1345-1346
- **Quoted text**: "$\Pr[y=1 \mid f(x)=0] = \eta$ (false positive)"
- **Problem**: The proof equates the false positive rate with $\eta$, but $\eta$ is defined as the average error rate over all in-domain elements (Definition 4.3). For Boolean-valued cipher maps, the false positive and false negative rates may differ.
- **Suggestion**: Add to the proposition statement: "Assume the cipher map has symmetric error: $\Pr[\text{dec}(\hat{f}(\text{enc}(x,k))) \neq f(x)] = \eta$ for both $f(x) = 0$ and $f(x) = 1$."

### m3. Abstract oversells construction layers (source: prose-auditor)
- **Location**: Abstract, lines 49-51
- **Quoted text**: "show how they arise from three orthogonal construction layers"
- **Problem**: The paper describes the construction layers as "conceptual scaffolding" (Section 3.3, line 274) and explicitly notes they may not satisfy formal algebraic laws. The abstract's phrasing implies a formal derivation.
- **Suggestion**: Change to "motivate them through three conceptual construction layers" or "trace their origin to three independent design choices."

### m4. Hash function $h$ defined twice with different domains (source: prose-auditor)
- **Location**: Line 455 ($h : \{0,1\}^* \to \{0,1\}^n$) and line 621 ($h : X^* \to \{0,1\}^n$)
- **Problem**: The hash function domain changes between constructions. Elements of $X^*$ are bit strings, so these are compatible, but the notation inconsistency is confusing.
- **Suggestion**: Define $h$ once in the ROM preliminaries paragraph (see M2) with domain $\{0,1\}^*$, and note that elements of any finite type are encoded as bit strings before hashing.

### m5. Space complexity proof conflates information content with physical storage (source: logic-checker)
- **Location**: Theorem 7.2, proof Step 1, lines 957-959
- **Quoted text**: "only $(1-\eta)n$ of the $n$ elements must decode correctly, reducing the effective element count"
- **Problem**: The $(1-\eta)$ factor represents a reduction in information-theoretic requirement, not a reduction in physical storage. The seed $\ell$ is a single value regardless of $\eta$.
- **Suggestion**: Clarify: "The $(1-\eta)$ factor reflects the reduced information content: permitting $\eta n$ failures removes $\eta n$ constraints from the seed search, requiring $\log_2(1/p)$ fewer bits of seed entropy."

### m6. Shannon's source coding theorem referenced but not cited (source: citation-verifier)

- **Problem**: "By Shannon's source coding theorem" appears twice without a citation.
- **Suggestion**: Add Shannon (1948) to the bibliography and cite it.

## Suggestions

1. **Include the existing figures.** The `img/fig_shmap.tex` and `img/fig_shs.tex` files exist but are not included. Add a diagram of the trusted/untrusted machine protocol flow (Section 6) and the Singular Hash Map construction (Section 7). (source: format-validator, prose-auditor)

2. **Add a worked example for the Singular Hash Map.** A small concrete example (e.g., 5 elements, $n=8$ bits) showing the seed search, prefix-free coding, and resulting cipher map would make the construction tangible. (source: methodology-auditor)

3. **Cite and discuss structured encryption (Chase & Kamara, 2010).** This is the closest existing framework to cipher maps. The paper should acknowledge the parallel and explain the differences (simulation-based security vs. information-theoretic parameters). (source: literature-context, novelty-assessor)

4. **Formalize the correctness definition with respect to query distribution.** Definition 4.3 uses the uniform distribution over $X$, but query distributions may be non-uniform. Note that the effective error rate under distribution $D$ is $\eta_D = \sum_x D(x) \cdot \mathbf{1}[\text{failing}(x)]$. (source: methodology-auditor)

5. **State practical bucket size limits for the entropy map.** The formalism document contains the formula $\ell_{\max} = \lfloor \log_2 T / \log_2(1/\varepsilon) \rfloor$ but the paper omits it. Including this would help practitioners. (source: methodology-auditor)

## Detailed Notes by Domain

### Logic and Proofs
The logical structure is sound. The composition theorem (Theorem 8.1) is the strongest result; its proof is correct under the stated re-randomization assumption. The lower bound proof (Theorem 7.1) is correct in its conclusion but has an informal step at $\varepsilon = n/|U|$. The Bayesian deniability proposition (Proposition 9.1) assumes symmetric error without stating it. The AND gate case analysis (Proposition 8.1) was verified correct. No fundamental logical errors were found.

### Novelty and Contribution
The primary contribution is the unifying abstraction itself -- packaging Bloom filters, perfect hashing, and bitwise set operations under one four-property framework with information-theoretic parameters. Individual ingredients are known; the packaging is new. The composition theorem formula is standard (independent error accumulation) but its application to cipher maps is the contribution. The encoding granularity principle (Section 9) is potentially the most original conceptual contribution but needs further development. Overall novelty is moderate, appropriate for a theory-oriented venue.

### Methodology
The paper is purely theoretical with no experiments, which is appropriate. Definitions are well-formed, constructions are clearly specified, and proofs establish their claims. The main methodological gap is the absence of a formal ROM assumption statement. Construction time analyses are honest about exponential costs but should be more explicit about practical feasibility limits. Algorithm 1 lacks a termination condition (infinite while loop).

### Writing and Presentation
The writing is clear, technically precise, and well-organized. The notation is consistent. The honest limitations sections are a strength. The main structural issue is section ordering (trust model after constructions). The abstract slightly oversells the construction layers. The "What This Framework Is Not" section in Section 10 duplicates the introduction. The paper would benefit from at least two figures to break up 20 pages of text.

### Citations and References
The bibliography is clean: 16 entries, all cited, no orphans, no undefined references. The most significant gap is the self-citation of an unpublished working paper [bernoulli-types] for the Bernoulli error model. Shannon (1948) and Bellare & Rogaway (1993) should be added. Structured encryption (Chase & Kamara, 2010) is the most important missing reference for the Related Work section.

### Formatting and Production
The paper builds cleanly (pdflatex + bibtex) with no errors and no undefined references. There are 9 warnings (6 cosmetic hyperref, 3 duplicate PDF destinations). The date is dynamic (\today). No venue-specific formatting is applied. Two TikZ figure files exist but are not included. The paper uses standard article class with 1-inch margins, which is appropriate for a working paper.

## Literature Context Summary

The paper's positioning is generally sound. The Related Work section covers the major lines of work: Bloom filters, perfect hashing, SSE, OPE/DET, garbled circuits, FHE, and homophonic substitution. The most significant literature gap is structured encryption (Chase & Kamara, 2010), which generalizes SSE to arbitrary data structures -- the closest existing parallel to cipher maps. The key differentiator (information-theoretic parameters vs. simulation-based security) is genuine and should be made explicit. Adding 2-3 references (Shannon 1948, Bellare-Rogaway 1993, Chase-Kamara 2010) would close the main gaps.
